MCT - Server/Client Rollback

source code

A networking experiment involving a larger than usual menu system, chat box, and rollback. Despite this faux game being a 2d, top-down type, I still tried to shoehorn rollback... realistically, I shouldn't have and for simplicity's sake should have used a low tick-rate TCP architecture instead.

Gaius - Crypto Bot

source code

Back in my day Bitcoin was $6.00... talk about a missed opportunity, eh? Gaius is a simple crypto bot that abides by the strategy of "whale trading" or in other words "volume trading". I don't know much about day trading, but others certainly seem to. So when Gaius spots a large buy/sell order it follows suit- piggybacking off other's hedges.

Halo History - Finding People You Knew

source code

A long time ago I played a lot of Halo on Xbox Live. That was very long ago and there are a lot of lost friends. So one day I decided to web scrape Bungie's player match history page and relearned the names of people I've played hundreds of games with. This web scraper scrapes all games and outputs the most played with players in descending order.

Mafia Message Saver - Cataloger

lost in time

I use to frequent EpicMafia, a deception based party game. It had a healthy community and many-a-players. But the site's future was iffy and that bothered me with how many people and memories I've shared through it so I made a program to scrape my entire DMs, organize them, format, and save locally as a stylized HTML file. It worked and I publically released it to the community.

And All the Rest

Most of the stuff I do is spurred from a desire to want something I can't easily find online or from curiosity of how it works:
- Reimplemented the server/host architecture in c# but with multi-thread support
- Multiple mock shopping sites with checkout, cookies, cart, and more
- A Pygame map maker. Import tiles to create, place them down in large worlds, and export a map

And lastly, what about those that didn't come to fruition?
- A peer to peer video streaming program so you and your buddies can watch videos together with your own bandwidth, no third party
- "Stream Events", an AI image recognition overlay for in-game events that can automatically trigger broadcaster settings
- "Spotify Together", reverse engineering Spotify I ran into issues. But the goal was to allow a program to snatch the current song and send that info to a peer also running the program that'll automatically switch their music so you can all listen together for free
